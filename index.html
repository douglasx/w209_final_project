Mass Shooting - What are the Facts



Modern Mass Shootings are happening every year and have increasing prevalence in the news. Due to coverage on only portions of the facts, news sources tend to bias and scare people into thinking mass shootings are happening more often in the recent years. Also, some biases are designed to target certain demographic group to where there are articles on “Are more mass shootings committed by white males?”Is this the fact?

Our purpose:

Create a interactive visualization to let you decide your own opinion and see unbiased facts/comparisons, though we will offer our insights as to why certain issues are more complicated.


Our Approach:


1. Modify Mass shooting definition

Mass shootings are defined by the FBI as "mass murder" as "four or more murdered during an event with no "cooling-off period" between the murders." However, this encapsulates a lot of other events that are not reported on in the media, so we’ve decided to provide visualizations which will allow you to enter your own definition of a mass murder.

2. Remove Mass shooting related biases

By defining your own way of looking at the mass shooting data, we are trying to remove the biases that news media usually have on mass shooting, the potential causes, and the potential fix to reduce the number of incidents.












Data:

  • Stanford MSA Database, which contains all the reported mass shooting events between 1960 and 2016. The database contains detailed description of the different aspects of the mass shooting events including: number of victims, shooter's description, weapon descriptions, etc.
  • US demographic data including the ethnicity distribution in the US from 1990 to 2016.
  • The firearms manufactured, imported and exported in the US from 1990 to 2016.

Users:


  • Gun control policy makers: The tool can be used by US gun control policy makers to look at the different aspects besides directly limiting the ownership and the sales of guns.
  • Gun control promoters and activists: Promoters and activists, including NGOs, can direct their focus on specific aspects of gun violence, and potentially offer viable solutions based on the insights from this tool.

Tasks:


1. Present the overview of mass shooting violence in the US, including changing the definition of mass shooting by changing the minimum number of victims.

2. Focus on the ethnicity distribution in the US population and the race of the shooter.

3. Focus on the firearms and weapon types.

    Insights:
  • Mass shootings where the shooter is African Americans are scattered more towards the East and South of the country.
  • There have been some US States where no mass shoooting was reported between 1990-2016.
  • The age groups 33-42 and 21-32 are the two dominant age groups of the shooter. However, additional data on the US age distribution is needed before concluding whether people of the age group have higher risk of causing mass shooting.
    Moreover, the age group with the highest number of reported mass shootings are slightly different across races.
