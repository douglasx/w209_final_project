Mass Shooting - What are the Facts?



Modern Mass Shootings are happening every year and have increasing coverage year over year. Due to how often it's been happening as of late, news sources tend to bias and scare people into thinking mass shootings are happening more often in the recent years. There are also some biases targeting certain demographics whenever these shootings occur such as white vs muslim extremism. But, is the media skewing the facts? What is the truth?

Our purpose:

Create an interactive visualization to let you answer any questions you have and see the unbiased data for yourself.


Our Approach:


1. Modify Mass shooting definition

Mass shootings are defined by the FBI as "mass murder" as "four or more murdered during an event with no "cooling-off period" between the murders." However, this encapsulates a lot of other events that are not reported by the media, so weâ€™ve decided to provide visualizations which will allow you to enter your own definition of a mass murder incident. Our dataset allows you to start from 3.

2. Remove Mass shooting related biases

By defining your own way of looking at the mass shooting data, we are trying to remove the biases that news media usually have on mass shooting, the potential causes, and the potential fix to reduce the number of incidents. However, we will also provide our own impartial insights to prevent any bias from forming within your minds.










Ethnicity and other facts

Below you will see a visualization that covers the following information
1. A stacked barchart showing all the shooting events from 1990 to 2016, with different color representing the race of the shooter.
2. Map of all the shooting events across the US, with size of circle representing total fatalities and color representing the shooter's race.
3. A scatter plot showing the role different gun types played in mass shooting.
4. A barchart showing the age distribution.
5. A bar chart showing the frequency of events during each day of a week.
